Municipality of Santa Cruz Proclamation

Santa Cruz, a first-class municipality in Davao del Sur, is known for its rich agricultural lands, diverse natural attractions, and vibrant cultural festivities. The municipality is blessed with scenic mountains, pristine beaches, and the majestic Mt. Apo that serves as a backdrop to this beautiful town.

Whereas, our rich cultural heritage and warm hospitality make us a premier tourist destination in the Davao region;

Now, therefore, be it proclaimed by the Municipal Government of Santa Cruz, Davao del Sur:

  • Santa Cruz celebrates its annual fiesta in honor of its patron saint, the Holy Cross (Santa Cruz), with cultural performances, traditional games, and community feasts showcasing local cuisine and delicacies.
  • The coastal areas of Santa Cruz shall be designated as protected ecotourism zones for sustainable development.
  • The historical sites and cultural landmarks shall receive special funding for preservation and promotion.
  • Tourism development funds shall be allocated to all barangays to support local initiatives.
  • Local artisans and cultural performers shall be recognized as "Santa Cruz Ambassadors" to promote our cultural heritage.

This proclamation takes effect immediately and shall guide our tourism development efforts for years to come.

Jaime M. Lao, Jr.

Municipal Mayor of Santa Cruz, Davao del Sur

Issued this 12th day of June, 2023

About Santa Cruz, Davao del Sur

Santa Cruz, officially the Municipality of Santa Cruz, is a first-class municipality in the province of Davao del Sur, Philippines. According to the 2020 census, it has a population of 95,004 people. The municipality is known for its agricultural productivity, particularly in coconut, banana, and rice farming.

Nestled at the foot of Mount Apo, the town offers both stunning mountain views and beautiful coastline along the Davao Gulf, making it a perfect destination for tourists seeking natural beauty and adventure.

Our Barangays

The Municipality of Santa Cruz is composed of 18 barangays, each with its own unique attractions and characteristics:

Astorga

Known for beautiful beaches and seaside resorts along the coast of Davao Gulf.

Bato

Features rich agricultural lands and traditional farming communities.

Coronon

Home to lush plantations and scenic rural landscapes.

Darong

Home to the famous Darong Hot Spring and agricultural plantations.

Inawayan

Coastal barangay with beautiful shorelines and fishing communities.

Jose Rizal (Kiblat)

Known for its agricultural productivity and historical landmarks.

Matutungan

Features diverse topography from hills to plains with vibrant communities.

Melilia

Known for local handicrafts and agricultural production.

Sibulan

Gateway to Mt. Apo trails and eco-tourism sites with lush forest landscapes.

Sinoron

Rural community with traditional farming practices and local festivals.

Tagabuli

Coastal area with mangrove forests and diverse marine ecosystems.

Tuban

Agricultural center with rich farmlands and irrigation systems.
